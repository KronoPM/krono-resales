How-to guide
Everything you need to handle any resale request
⚠ Critical rules — always apply
No work begins until certified check payment is received and confirmed by Radu.
PAL requires both: completed Buyer Info Form AND fully executed contract of sale. No exceptions.
RFR associations: board must waive right of first refusal. Submit at least 30 days before closing.
Check condo insurance is not expired before including in any package.
↻ If closing is under 30 days and association has RFR — alert Radu immediately.
↻ Lender may send more pages than paid for — confirm payment before completing extra pages.
ℹ All inquiries → resales@kronopm.com or 847-657-9977, ask for Radu.
1
Receive & log the inquiry
▼

Attorney or lender emails resales@kronopm.com. Log it in this tracker immediately as a new request with status Awaiting payment. Note exactly what they are asking for — closing package, PAL, lender questionnaire, or a combination. Note the closing date.

⚠ If closing is less than 30 days away and the association has RFR — contact Radu immediately. Timeline may already be too tight.
ℹ Any employee who receives a resale inquiry must forward it to resales@kronopm.com — do not try to handle it yourself.
2
Send the cover letter & fee schedule
▼

Reply with the standard Krono cover letter attached. It explains all packages, rush fees, and payment instructions. Use the Email templates in this app to send the initial response.

Certified check only, payable to Krono Property Management, mailed to:

960 Rand Road, Suite 211
Des Plaines, IL 60016
Attention: Radu Dulu
3
Wait for payment — do nothing until confirmed
▼

Do nothing until Radu physically confirms the certified check has been received. Once confirmed, log the payment in the tracker and change status to In progress.

The 10-business-day clock starts from payment receipt — not from the inquiry date.

⚠ Never start preparing any documents before Radu confirms payment received.
↻ Rush orders received after business hours or on holidays — clock starts next business day.
4
Use the smart checklist
▼

Click the request in the tracker to open the smart checklist. It shows only the steps relevant to what was ordered and automatically flags any special rules for that association — RFR, interview required, expired insurance warning, board approval, rental restrictions.

Work through each step in order and check off as you go. The progress bar on the dashboard card updates in real time.

5
Closing package — $375
▼

Pull together and send to the requesting attorney:

  • Condo Declaration & Bylaws
  • Rules & Regulations
  • Current Budget
  • Meeting Minutes (past year)
  • Condo Insurance Policy (verify not expired first)
  • 22.1 Disclosure Form (most critical — fill carefully)
⚠ Check insurance expiry before including it. Expired insurance in a closing package causes serious problems at closing.
6
PAL — Paid Assessment Letter — $150
▼

Before preparing the PAL you must have from the requester:

  • Completed Buyer Information Form
  • Fully executed contract of sale

Check the seller account for delinquent assessments — the PAL cannot show a clean balance if there are unpaid dues.

If the association has Right of First Refusal — submit the waiver request to the Board at least 30 days before closing and wait for written confirmation before releasing.

If the association requires a buyer interview — coordinate scheduling with the Board early.

⚠ Never release PAL without both the buyer info form AND the executed contract. Both required, no exceptions.
7
Lender questionnaire — $200+
▼

Receive the questionnaire from the lender. Count the pages before doing anything.

  • First 2 pages: $200
  • Each additional page: +$50

Confirm the payment received matches the actual page count. Fill all pages thoroughly and attach the condo insurance policy. Send completed questionnaire back to the lender's specific contact.

↻ If lender sends more pages than paid for — confirm additional payment before completing extra pages.
8
Deliver & close out
▼

Confirm all ordered documents delivered to the correct parties. If attorney and lender are both involved — closing package to attorney, lender Q to lender. Do not mix them up.

Update status to Completed in the tracker and log the completion date.

ℹ If a lender changes mid-process, you may need to re-issue certain documents. Check with Radu.
